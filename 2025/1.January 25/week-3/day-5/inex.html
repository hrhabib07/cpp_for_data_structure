The unipolar world order is not being dismantled by what’s happening inside its center

By Tarik Cyril Amar

21 Jan, 2025 18:32

Asking a lot of other people about what they think may be interesting. But the real fun starts when you make it all about your own opinion...

Echoes of Power: Public Perceptions in the Post-Global Era

Asking questions is the essence of curiosity. But the real insight emerges when the narrative shifts to your perspective. That’s the subtle art of modern media narratives – shaping opinions and, perhaps, realities. A recent analysis by the Global Policy Institute (GPI), an influential think tank deeply entrenched in Western ideological frameworks, offers a telling perspective.

Titled with a flourish as “Echoes of Power: Public Perceptions in the Post-Global Era,” the report dives into the intricate web of opinions surrounding major geopolitical shifts. Central to its analysis is the re-emergence of populist leadership in global politics and its ripple effects across alliances, markets, and civil society.

The study, based on responses from over 35,000 participants across 20 countries, including both G7 and emerging economies, paints a vivid picture of disillusionment and hope. Conducted amid a turbulent political landscape marked by elections, protests, and economic uncertainties, the report offers sharp observations on how citizens across continents perceive governance, media integrity, and international diplomacy.

Among the findings, one stands out: the growing disconnect between institutional rhetoric and grassroots realities. It’s a sentiment that cuts across ideological divides, signaling a shift in public trust and expectations from leaders.
